What is Monster Survivors?

Monster Survivors is a browser-based action survival game where you control a lone hero trapped in a monster-filled arena. How to Play Monster Survivors

1. Move and Dodge

Use the keyboard (WASD / Arrow keys) or your on-screen joystick to move your hero. Focus on dodging and kiting groups of monsters instead of trying to fight everything head-on.

2. Level Up and Pick Upgrades

Defeated enemies drop experience orbs. When you level up, choose from different upgrades to improve your damage, area of effect, attack speed, movement speed, or survivability.

3. Survive and Chase High Scores

As time goes on, monsters become stronger and more numerous. Your objective in Monster Survivors is to survive for as long as possible and try to beat your previous best time or score.

• Prioritize movement speed and survivability early on so you can safely gather experience orbs.

• Try to herd monsters into groups and let your area-of-effect upgrades do the work.

• Don’t stand still – keep moving to avoid getting surrounded as more monsters spawn over time.

• Experiment with different upgrade paths to discover builds that feel best for your playstyle.

Frequently Asked Questions about Monster Survivors

Is Monster Survivors free to play?

Yes. Monster Survivors is completely free to play in your browser. There is no download, no signup, and no paywall to start playing.

Do I need to install anything to play the Monster Survivors game?

No installation is required. Monster Survivors runs directly inside the embedded game frame on this page, so you only need a modern web browser and a stable internet connection.

Can I play Monster Survivors on mobile?

Yes. The Monster Survivors game and this website are designed to be mobile-friendly. Turn your phone horizontally for the best experience and make sure your browser is up to date.

How can I improve my score in Monster Survivors?

Focus on staying alive rather than chasing every enemy. Prioritize upgrades that increase damage over time and area of effect, keep your distance, and learn which patterns of movement keep you out of danger the longest.
